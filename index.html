How it works

AD-MPCC combines two components operating at each time step:

  • Online Parameter Estimation — a prior-regularized moving-horizon estimator (MHE) with exponentially decaying weights rapidly updates Pacejka tire parameters to capture surface transitions in real time.
  • Differentiable MPCC — compute the sensitivity of the MPCC solution w.r.t. objective weights via the implicit function theorem (IFT) to find optimal MPCC weights.
  • Pacejka-informed ML — a Pacejka-informed machine learning model trained offline to approximate these optimal weights from a 5-dimensional input, enabling real-time weight adaptation.
// simulation results

Single-Surface Scenario

All four controllers are evaluated on a uniform road surface with μmax = 1.2 for 10 laps. Diff-MPCC and AD-MPCC achieve approximately 11 s faster lap times than the baseline MPCC by adapting the objective weights online, while AD-MPCC further maintains the smallest mean lateral offset (0.926 m), demonstrating a better safety–speed trade-off.

Multi-Surface Scenario

The track surface varies from μmax = 0.7 to 1.2 across different sections. Only AD-MPCC successfully completes the lap by jointly updating Pacejka parameters and MPCC weights in real time, while MPCC, A-MPCC, and Diff-MPCC all crash at the first slippery segment (μmax = 0.75) due to insufficient adaptation.
